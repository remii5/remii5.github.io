Facial Recognition Door Lock

Problem

Taking my keys, remembering to not lose my keys, and opening the door with my keys got quite annoying and boring. So, with machine learning, a servo motor, camera, and raspberry pi, I wanted to design a system that would automate my door lock via facial recognition.

There were several challenges to overcome. Firstly, I needed to configure a library that could recognize my face and send a message to my motor to turn a certain degree. My solution was to use a popular github repo for the library and store photos I have of myself into the file system. I programmed the camera to take periodic snapshots of what it was seeing and compared it to my library. If there was a match the motor was signaled to either open or close the door lock. Another issue was finding the right motor. Knowing that motor type - servo - would be the best fit for precise turning, researching a catalogue of motors was necessary. My first choice had a weaker output of torque so I had to upgrade to what is shown in my final setup.

Results

The results above show the results from the facial recognition library and snapshots from my camera. The "known" photos I decided to give the raspberry pi had a mixture of me wearing both glasses or not wearing glasses. This allowed me to have successful results whenever I chose to wear the lens. The motor was able to both lock and unlock my door allowing me to access my room at will.

Reflections

Prior to this project I had never gotten to have hands on experience with some of these tools before. Using machine learning and working with a raspberry pi was completely new to me and was an amazing experience. Finally having an opportunity to get started in the field of engineering was a good way for me to narrow down what I am liking or disliking. From this project alone I have been able to realize that the world Machine Learning, and Digital Systems would be an interesting avenue to look into.
